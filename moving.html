Enjoying the life in Perth and ready to make the move? You are not alone. For the past years, Perth is experiencing significant population growth. Perth population was 14,186 people in 2006, and it has been growing to an estimated 28,000 people in 2018 (Remplan Community, 2019).

The majority of Perth city residents are single and young couples, between 20 to 34 years old (Remplan Community, 2019). The number of the younger residents in Perth city is significantly higher than Perth region in average (Remplan Community, 2019).

Below are some handy information that will hopefully help you to settle sooner in the heart of WA.

Location

Postcode: 6000

Nearby landmarks: Swan River, Kings Park and Bell Tower.

Nearby suburbs: North Perth, South Perth, East Perth, West Perth, Northbridge, Subiaco, Victoria Park and Leederville.

Prices

Rent
  • Average cost to rent a unit: $400/week, with price from $340/week for 1 bedroom unit to $580 for 3 bedroom unit (Realestate.com.au, 2019).
  • Average cost to rent a house: $443/week, with price from $425/week for 2 bedroom house to $498 for 3 bedroom house (Realestate.com.au, 2019).
  • Rent price data last updated: 31 May 2019.
Buy
  • Average cost to own a house: $840,000, with price from $816,500 for 2 bedroom house (Realestate.com.au, 2019).
  • Average cost to own a unit: $402,000, with price from $300,000 for 1 bedroom unit to $520,000 for 3 bedroom unit (Realestate.com.au, 2019).
  • Buy price data last updated: 28 May 2019.

Key facts

Local schools Mercedes Ladies College, North Perth P.S., Alexander Technology Institute (Reiwa, 2019)
Nearby universities University of Western Australia
Hospitals Royal Perth Hospital Wellington Street Campus, Mount Hospital, South Perth Hospital
Transportations Perth Central, Perth Underground, Perth Bus Station, Elizabeth Quay Busport (Reiwa, 2019)
Shops Carillion City, Forrest Chase, Hay Street Mall, Murray Street Mall (Reiwa, 2019)
